------------------------------

Subject: Model 86-B "Zone Terminal"
Location: Sector 3 (Decommissioned)

I. Power-On Sequence

Upon initialization, the terminal will perform a sector scan. Do not interrupt the boot sequence. If the blinking cursor halts for more than 5 seconds, the hardware is likely experiencing physical decay from local interference.

II. Atmospheric Monitoring (The LED Meter)

The LED array is your only reliable metric for local stability.

Green/Yellow: Standard background interference.
Red (Pulsing): Ambient radiation exceeds safety thresholds. Limit exposure time.

III. ACOUSTIC DAMPENING (DANGER OF COGNITIVE HAZARD)

The background drones are a byproduct of the turbine's residual resonance. While they provide data on "Zone activity", prolonged exposure is known to cause discomfort. Use the Physical Switch to dampen the signal if you begin to hear voices within the static.

IV. SECTOR SURVEILLANCE (CHANNEL LOGS)

Navigate channels with extreme caution. The signal is a direct window into the Zone.

  • CH 01 (Intake): Safe for long-term observation.
  • CH 02: Structural integrity monitor. Watch for non-mechanical movement within the machinery shadows.
  • CH 03 (Core): STRICTLY PROHIBITED FOR PROLONGED VIEWING. Hardware failure is imminent upon connection. The signal distortion here is not technical; it is the visual manifestation of the Core's influence.

V. CLINICAL MANIFESTATIONS OF PROLONGED EXPOSURE

Personnel remaining in Sector 3 beyond the allotted 15-minute window may exhibit the following symptoms:

  • Acoustic Pareidolia (Phase I): The operator begins to hear structured language or familiar voices within the background drone of the turbines. If the terminal speaks your name, engage the Acoustic Dampening switch immediately.
  • Chronological Displacement (Phase II): A breakdown of the internal clock. Operators may perceive seconds as hours or find themselves anticipating visual glitches on the CRT before they occur.
  • The "Glass Wall" Effect (Phase III): An acute feeling of being watched by the machine itself. This is often accompanied by the sensation that the image on the display is slowly approaching from the distance.
  • Shadow Integration (Phase IV): The inability to distinguish between the physical shadows in the room and the digital artifacts on the screen.

WARNING: If you experience a metallic taste in your mouth or find that you can no longer remember your home address, disconnect the terminal power source and report to the decontamination unit.

DO NOT LOOK DIRECTLY INTO THE CORE IF THE SCREEN BEGINS TO BLEED.
